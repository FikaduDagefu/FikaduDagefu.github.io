Research Overview

I serve as a Team Leader and Senior Research Scientist at the U.S. Army Research Laboratory (ARL). Broadly, my research interests lie in applied electromagnetics, wireless communications, and sensing, with a recent emphasis on developing enabling technologies for intelligent, self-managing wireless networks. I have led multiple intramural research programs and collaborated extensively with academia and other government agencies (such as DARPA). To date, I have authored or co-authored over 120 peer-reviewed publications and hold six patents. Additionally, I regularly mentor student interns, postdocs, and visiting scholars on my research team at ARL.

Research Areas

  • Wireless Communications and Sensing: Intelligent networks, Covert communications, Optimization techniques
  • Antennas and RF Technologies: Small and non-Foster antennas, Diversity antennas, Reconfigurable intelligent Surfaces
  • Wave Propagation and Channel Modeling: RF wave propagation modeling, Optical channel modeling, Empirical models
