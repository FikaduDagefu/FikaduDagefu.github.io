Research Overview

Welcome to my academic and professional portfolio. I am a Senior Research Scientist at [National Laboratory] with over 13 years of post-PhD experience.

My primary research focuses on the intersection of high-performance computing, advanced materials characterization, and machine learning applications for national security challenges. I currently lead multi-disciplinary teams working to transition fundamental science into applied mission capabilities.

Current Focus Areas

  • High-Performance Computing: Scaling physics-based simulations for exascale architectures.
  • AI/ML Integration: Applying physics-informed neural networks to sparse experimental datasets.

Professional Biography

received the B.S. degree in electrical engineering from The University of Texas at Austin, and the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in electrical engineering from the University of Michigan. He is currently the Team Leader and a Senior Research Scientist with U.S. Army Research Laboratory, where he has authored over 110 peer-reviewed publications and holds six patents. He has led numerous intramural research programs and has collaborated extensively with DARPA, AFRL, and C5ISR, notably serving as the ARL Team Leader for V&V on DARPA’s NGEST and RN DMC programs. His research interests include wireless channel modeling, antennas, PHY layer security, and intelligent heterogeneous networks. He has received best paper awards at the 2019 ICMCIS and the 2025 IEEE MILCOM Conference. He received the ARL Brad Forch Mentorship Award in 2024 and the Department of the Army Civilian Service Commendation Award in 2022. He serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE ANTENNAS AND WIRELESS PROPAGATION LETTERS, IEEE Antennas and Propagation Magazine, and Radio Science Letters..
